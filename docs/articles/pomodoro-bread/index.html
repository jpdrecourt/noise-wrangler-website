The Pomodoro Bread Recipe

A sourdough calculator for the work-from-home artisan

Time-box your bread making using the Pomodoro Technique. Each pomodoro represents 25 minutes of focused work or rest. Perfect for fitting bread making into your remote work routine.

Equipment You'll Need

  • Large mixing bowl - for mixing and fermenting the dough
  • Banneton or proofing basket - or use a bowl lined with a well-floured tea towel
  • Dutch oven with lid - for baking (cast iron or enamel)
  • Kitchen scale - essential for accuracy
  • Bench scraper - helpful for shaping (optional)
  • Bread lame or sharp knife - for scoring

Maintaining Your Starter Between Bakes

The no-waste approach: Most sourdough guides tell you to feed and discard regularly. This method wastes nothing and keeps your starter dormant in the fridge until you need it.

After Each Bake
  1. You've just used 200g of starter in your bread
  2. Add 100g flour to the remains in your jar (no water)
  3. Mix thoroughly until you get a dry, powdery mixture
  4. Store in the fridge - the dry powder stays dormant, no feeding needed

Why it works: Without water, fermentation stops. No waste, no guilt, no constant feeding.

24 Hours Before Your Next Bake
  1. Take your jar from the fridge
  2. Add 100g water (matching the flour for 100% hydration)
  3. Mix well - it will become a thick, active starter
  4. Place in a warm spot (~25¬∞C) for 24 hours
  5. Your starter is ready when it's bubbly and doubled in size
Scaling Up or Down

Need more starter? Add extra flour to the remains before storing. Then add the same amount of water when activating (e.g., 150g flour ‚Üí 150g water for 300g total).

Need less? Use less of your activated starter in the recipe, or adjust the starter percentage in the calculator above.

Starting From Scratch

If you don't have a starter yet, mix 100g flour + 100g water. Keep at room temperature, feeding daily (discard half, add 50g flour + 50g water) for 5-7 days until active and bubbly. Then switch to this no-waste method.

Notes

  • 1 pomodoro = 25 minutes of focused time
  • Temperature ranges: Room temp 18-22¬∞C, Cold fermentation 3-5¬∞C
  • Target internal temperature for baked bread: 98-100¬∞C
  • If baking from cold, add 5 minutes to covered baking time
