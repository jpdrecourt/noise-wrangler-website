The AI Archetype Council

Multiple AI agents, each rooted in a different intellectual tradition, challenge your thinking in parallel. The voices evolve with every session.

Get it

  1. Download from github.com/jpdrecourt/AI-Archetype-Council
  2. Copy the commands/ and skills/ folders into your project's .claude/ directory (or ~/.claude/ for global access)
  3. Run /council Your topic here

MIT licensed. Built for Claude Code, adaptable to any multi-agent AI setup.

What it does

You give the council a topic. A Convener selects 3-4 voices from a pool of ten archetypes and spawns them as persistent teammates using Claude Code's Agent Teams. Each voice runs independently, with its own worldview and its own context. The same question looks different to The Engineer (systems, constraints, feedback loops) and The Hermit (what if you did less?).

Each turn has two phases. First, every voice asks for material relevant to its tradition: The Craftsperson wants to see the prototype, The Critic wants references. The Convener fulfils those requests, then voices respond. Because each voice asked for different things, each enters the discussion having read different parts of the project. This information asymmetry is what produces genuine diversity of perspective.

When disagreement surfaces, voices debate directly, challenging, building on, or contesting each other's claims. You react, they respond again, turn by turn.

The voices

  • Engineer: systems thinking, cybernetics, constraints
  • Alchemist: transformation stages, what must break down first
  • Craftsperson: embodied practice, prototyping, material resistance
  • Critic: art-historical context, lineage, who came before
  • Child: pre-categorical perception, "but why?"
  • Griot: oral tradition, cycles, the story underneath
  • Hermit: subtraction, silence, what's already sufficient
  • Witness: phenomenological and animist attention, what is present here
  • Fence: political economy of culture, value flows, extraction

Voices that evolve

Each voice has a scratchpad: private notes that accumulate across sessions. Raw material, unresolved tensions, things that couldn't be said in the moment. At session close, voices can compost their scratchpads, promoting recurring patterns into their voice definition and pruning what has been fully absorbed.

This builds something like personality over time. After 20 sessions, The Craftsperson knows when a limitation lives in the design, not in the hands. The Convener knows that philosophical insights work better reframed as design methodology. Your council diverges from mine, shaped by different questions and different corrections.

Make it yours

These voices serve my practice as a sound artist and generative systems designer. Swap them. Give them names. Make them domain-specific. Add new voices by dropping a markdown file in the voices/ folder. The format is simple: a tradition, an attention pattern, a distrust, a core question, and a couple of paragraphs on how the voice thinks.

Cost

Each voice runs with a compact prompt (around 800 words) and returns 2-3 sentences per turn. Voices run independently, each with a small context window. Only the Convener accumulates the full session history. A typical 4-5 turn session with 3-4 voices costs around 100-150k tokens.
